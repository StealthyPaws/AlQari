Al-Lisaan refers to the tongue, while Al-Asnaan are the teeth, we will study both to pronounce letters pertaining to these categories. There are ten articulation points for eighteen letters. 
These ten articulation points are distributed over four
areas of the tongue:
1- Aqsa Al-Lisaan: The deepest part of the tongue
2- Wast al-Lisaan: The middle of the tongue
3- Haafat al-Lisaan: The edges (sides) of the tongue
4- Taraf al-Lisaan: The region of the tongue from canine to canine
5- Ra’s al-Lisaan: The very tip of the tongue

The roof of the mouth is divided into 2 parts:
- The hard palate; the top roof area, near the teeth.
- The soft palate; deepest part of the roof, near the throat.

The tongue touches the gums or the hard or the soft
palates to produce different letters.


The deepest part of the tongue:
قاف – كاف
These letters are pronounced from the deepest part of the tongue along with the roof of the mouth. These are called Huroof Lihatiyya.

The middle of the tongue:
جيم – شين – ياء
These letters are called Huroof Al-Shajariyya and are pronounced from the middle of the tongue and what lies opposite to it in the palate.

The edges of the tongue:
ضاد
This letter is called Hurf Al-Hafiyya and is pronounced from the edges (sides) of the tongue along with the upper molars.

The region of the tongue from canine to canine:
نون – راء – لام
These letters are referred to as Huroof Aṭ-Ṭarafiyyah / Az-Zalaqiyyah pronounced from the front region of the tongue extending from one canine to the other.

The tip of the tongue:
(a) Huroof An-Nitiyyah (حروف نِطْيّة): 
ت – د – ط
These are pronounced with the tip of the tongue against the roots of the front teeth.

(b) Huroof As-Safeeriyyah (حروف صفيرية):
 س – ز – ص
These are pronounced with a sharp hissing/whistling sound from the tip of the tongue.

(c) Huroof Al-Lithawiyyah (حروف لثوية): 
ث – ذ – ظ
These are pronounced with the tip of the tongue touching the edges of the upper front teeth.
