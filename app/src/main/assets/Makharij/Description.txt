The point articulation (Makhrij/الْمَخْرَجُ):
It is the place from where a letter is pronounced, making its sound different from the sound of other letters. Using the right articulation point of a letter is necessary to utter the letter correctly.

The sound (Saut/الْصَوْتُ):
It is a group of vibrations and waves carried in the air to
the human ears.

The letter (Huroof/الْحَرْفُ):
It is a sound that is pronounced from a specific
articulation point.


The speech system is divided into five major areas. Each letter has one articulation point, which is used to produce the sound of the letter. Some articulation points have more than one spot which produces different sounds. There are 17 different articulation points to pronounce the 28 Arabic letters.

The following are the five major areas of the speech
system:
1- Al-Jawf (The empty space in the mouth and throat): It has one articulation point for the three letters of Madd.
2- Al-Halq (The Throat): 
It has three articulation points for six different letters which are pronounced from the deepest, middle, and closest part of the throat.
3- Al-Lisaan (The Tongue):
It has ten articulation points for eighteen letters.
4- Ash-Shufataan (The Two Lips): 
The lips have two articulation points for four letters.
5- Al-Khashum (The Nasal Cavity): 
It is a large air-filled space above and behind the nose, in the middle of the face. It is the continuation of both nostrils. There is one articulation point.
